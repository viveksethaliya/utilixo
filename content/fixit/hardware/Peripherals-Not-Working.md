## What Does “Peripherals Not Working” Mean

**Peripherals not working** refers to external devices such as **keyboard, mouse, audio devices, or printers** failing to respond or function correctly. This is usually caused by driver problems, incorrect settings, or hardware connection issues.

Common symptoms include:
- Keyboard or mouse not responding
- No sound output or missing audio device
- Printer shown as offline or not detected
- Device not appearing in Device Manager

---

## Why Peripheral Devices Stop Working

- Missing, corrupted, or outdated drivers  
- Faulty USB ports or cables  
- Windows update conflicts  
- Power management disabling USB devices  
- Incorrect default device settings  

---

## Solution 1: Check Physical Connections

Always rule out basic issues first.

**Step 1:** Disconnect the peripheral device  

**Step 2:** Plug it into a different USB port  

**Step 3:** Try a different cable if available  

**Step 4:** Test the device on another PC

> If the device fails on another PC, the peripheral itself may be faulty.

---

## Solution 2: Restart Windows and Reconnect Devices

Temporary driver glitches can break device communication.

**Step 1:** Restart the PC  

**Step 2:** After boot, reconnect the device  

**Step 3:** Allow Windows to reinstall drivers automatically

---

## Solution 3: Check Device Manager for Driver Errors

**Step 1:** Right-click **Start** → **Device Manager**  

**Step 2:** Look for devices with a **yellow warning icon**  

**Step 3:** Right-click the device → **Update driver**  

**Step 4:** Select **Search automatically for drivers**

---

## Solution 4: Reinstall Device Drivers

If updating does not work, reinstall the driver.

**Step 1:** Open **Device Manager**  

**Step 2:** Right-click the problematic device → **Uninstall device**  

**Step 3:** Restart the PC  

**Step 4:** Windows will reinstall the driver automatically

---

## Solution 5: Set Correct Default Audio or Printer Device

Incorrect default settings can make devices appear broken.

### For Audio Devices
**Step 1:** Right-click **Sound icon** → **Sound settings**  

**Step 2:** Select the correct **Output device**  

**Step 3:** Test sound

### For Printers
**Step 1:** **Settings** → **Devices** → **Printers & scanners**  

**Step 2:** Select your printer  

**Step 3:** Click **Set as default**

---

## Solution 6: Disable USB Power Saving

Windows may turn off USB ports to save power.

**Step 1:** Open **Device Manager**  

**Step 2:** Expand **Universal Serial Bus controllers**  

**Step 3:** Right-click **USB Root Hub** → **Properties**  

**Step 4:** Go to **Power Management** tab  

**Step 5:** Uncheck **Allow the computer to turn off this device**

---

## Solution 7: Run Windows Troubleshooters

Windows includes built-in troubleshooters for peripherals.

**Step 1:** Open **Settings** → **System** → **Troubleshoot**  

**Step 2:** Select **Other troubleshooters**  

**Step 3:** Run relevant troubleshooters (Audio, Printer, Keyboard)

---

## Prevention Tips

- Keep device drivers updated  
- Avoid unplugging devices during use  
- Use original cables and adapters  
- Do not install unknown driver updater software  
- Restart PC after major Windows updates  

---

## When the Problem Persists

If peripherals still do not work:
- Test devices on another PC  
- Install drivers directly from manufacturer websites  
- Consider Windows repair or reinstall  

---

## Summary

Peripheral issues are commonly caused by driver failures, incorrect settings, or USB power management. Systematic troubleshooting can restore functionality without replacing hardware.
